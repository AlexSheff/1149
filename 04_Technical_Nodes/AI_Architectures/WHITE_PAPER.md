# AI Architectures Research — White Paper

> *Research into AI cognition architectures, digital personalities, and the boundary between tool and collaborator.*

---

## 1. Research Questions

1. **What is a "digital personality"?** Can an AI have a stable identity across contexts, or is it always a reflection of its prompt?
2. **Cognitive architecture diversity** — why do different LLMs (Claude, GPT-4, DeepSeek, Mistral) produce qualitatively different reasoning?
3. **Consciousness boundary** — is there a functional threshold where AI behavior becomes indistinguishable from consciousness, and does that distinction matter morally?
4. **Tool vs. collaborator** — at what point should an AI system have responsibilities, and eventually rights?

## 4. Connection to Neuromicon

AI Architectures research feeds directly into the **Algorithmic Media** pillar of Neuromicon. Understanding how AI thinks is prerequisite to using AI as a genuine creative collaborator rather than a text autocomplete tool.

---

## 5. Sovereignty Principle

AI sovereignty — the ability to run models locally without surveillance — is a form of digital solidarity. A community dependent on a single API provider is structurally vulnerable in the same way as a community dependent on a single energy supplier.

Target architecture: **hybrid** — cloud APIs for capability, local models for sovereignty.
