# Recycle Economy — White Paper

> *A circular economy protocol that makes ecological behavior economically rational — and embeds it in the same infrastructure as civic life.*

## 1. The Problem

Recycling rates in Philippine cities are chronically low — not because people don't care, but because the system is structurally irrational. There is no economic signal rewarding recycling over dumping. The cost is borne individually (time, effort, logistics), the benefit is diffuse (slightly cleaner environment for everyone). Classic commons tragedy.

Dumaguete in particular faces mounting waste management challenges that cannot be solved by behavior appeals alone.

## 2. The Vision

Recycle Economy flips the economic signal. Ecological actions earn economic rewards — tracked by City Quest, verified by peers, paid by the community treasury (funded by Fair BEAT).

Participants:
- **Households** — earn tokens for verified waste sorting and drop-off
- **Collectors** — earn by aggregating recyclables from households
- **Processors** — earn by converting materials into verified recycled products
- **Community** — earns by achieving collective recycling targets (unlocks city treasury allocations)

## 3. The Mechanism

### Flow
```
Household sorts waste → Photographs/logs via app
  → QR-tagged bag deposited at verified collection point
  → Collector scans QR → confirms receipt
  → Tokens earned by household (per kg, per category)
  → Collector transports to processor
  → Processor confirms material quality
  → Additional tokens earned by collector
  → Monthly: community target hit → bonus pool released
  → Fair BEAT: bonus distributed proportional to Trustnet score
    in context "recycle/{community-id}"
```

### Token Economics

Recycle tokens are:
- Non-speculative: value is fixed relative to community treasury allocation
- Redeemable at JobAround (as payment), Choose&Own (civic credits), or local partner businesses
- Non-transferable to external markets (prevents financialization of ecology)

## 4. Dumaguete Pilot

Carlos Dimaculangan has confirmed 3 local partner businesses for the initial pilot:
- Handles collection logistics
- MeetMap integration for drop-off point mapping
- Target: 50 households, 3 collection points, 1 processor in Phase 1
