# ImInGame — White Paper

> *Civic participation as a game layer — making the invisible visible, and the mundane meaningful.*

## 1. The Problem

Civic life is full of small but important actions that go unrecognized: picking up litter, helping a neighbor, attending a community event, contributing to a local decision. These actions exist but are invisible to the community and unrewarded by any system.

At the same time, people respond strongly to game mechanics: progress bars, streaks, achievements, leaderboards. Games are not frivolous — they are the most successful engagement systems humans have built.

The insight: civic life can use the same mechanics.

## 2. The Vision

ImInGame is the gamification layer of City Quest. It does not replace the substantive apps (Choose&Own, JobAround, Guardians) — it makes their activity visible, trackable, and rewarding as a unified experience.

Think of it as: all City Quest actions → consolidated into a single "civic RPG" where your character grows through real-world contributions.

## 3. The Mechanism

### Civic Quests

```
Quest: "Clean Canal Day"
  Location: MeetMap coordinates
  Required: Show up + log 2hr participation
  Reward: +5 civic stake + "Canal Guardian" badge + 50 Fair BEAT tokens
  Participants: 12/20 joined · Quest active
```

### Character Progression

Each citizen has a civic profile with:
- Level (1–100, based on cumulative action points)
- Specializations: Protector / Builder / Creator / Connector / Observer
- Badges: verifiable on-chain achievements
- Active quests: up to 5 simultaneously
- Streak: consecutive days with at least one civic action

### Feed

Real-time community activity feed — what Guardians responded to, what Recycle Economy drop-offs happened, who just completed a Choose&Own task. Makes civic activity visible and social.
