# Duma inWork — White Paper

> *The bridge between City Quest's digital governance layer and Dumaguete's real civic infrastructure.*

## 1. The Vision

Duma inWork is the local governance integration module of City Quest — built specifically for Dumaguete, but designed to be deployed in any Philippine city.

Where Choose&Own handles community-level micro-governance, Duma inWork operates at the barangay and city level: connecting City Quest participants with actual government processes, making them visible, and enabling participation where the formal system allows it.

## 2. What It Does

### Governance Transparency Layer
- Scrapes/integrates public barangay council agendas and minutes
- Makes them searchable, subscribable, and commentable by citizens
- Tracks which issues have been raised by citizens vs. initiated by officials

### Participation Bridge
- Citizens can file formal community input on open agenda items
- Input is Trustnet-signed (verifiable as coming from a real, accountable community member)
- Input is aggregated and scored by community weight (Choose&Own stake)

### Local Budget Visualization
- Where does the barangay budget go?
- Which line items are underspent / overspent?
- Community annotation layer: "this road has been 'under repair' for 3 years"

## 3. Core Philosophy

- **Not a replacement for government** — a transparency and participation layer alongside it
- **Accountability through visibility** — what gets measured, gets managed
- **Dignity of process** — formal participation pathways, not just noise

## 4. Dumaguete First

Dumaguete is chosen as the pilot for specific reasons:
- Manageable size (city of ~130,000)
- Active UU community network with existing civic relationships
- Good Project Corp has local organizational capacity
- Carlos Dimaculangan has business council relationships
