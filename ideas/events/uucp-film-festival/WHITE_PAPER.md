# UUCP Film Festival — White Paper

> *An independent film festival rooted in Dumaguete, programmed by community, distributed globally via FilmFreeway.*

## 1. Overview

The UUCP (Unitarian Universalist Community Philippines) Film Festival is a recurring cultural event in Dumaguete that screens independent short and feature films, with an emphasis on:
- Community-generated content (local and international)
- Ethical and civic themes aligned with UU values
- Emerging Filipino and Southeast Asian cinema
- AI-augmented filmmaking (growing category as of Season 3)

**Season status:** Season 3 underway. Season 2 (Feb–Mar 2026 Dumaguete event) completed successfully.

## 2. Model

### Curation
- Primary curator: Elena Vasquez
- Submissions: FilmFreeway international circuit
- Selection: trust-weighted community panel (5 curators minimum)
- Split target: 60% international / 40% local + regional

### Programming
- 4–6 screening events per season
- Venue: Dumaguete Cultural Center + partner spaces
- Online streaming component: selected films available 30 days post-screening

### Economics
- Entry fees (FilmFreeway): ~$10–25/submission
- Ticket revenue: community split — 50% venue/ops / 30% filmmaker honoraria / 20% City Quest treasury
- Sponsorship: UU community network + local business partners

## 3. AI Cinema Category (Season 3 New)

A new dedicated programming track for films made with AI tools:
- Minimum 30% of visual content AI-generated
- Attribution mandatory: which tools, which human direction
- Judged separately from traditional shorts
- AI Cinema Lab is the Dumaguete incubator for this category

## 4. FilmFreeway Integration

All submissions managed via FilmFreeway:
- Festival profile maintained year-round
- International distribution: ~200 submissions/season target
- Laurels: official festival selection, jury awards
