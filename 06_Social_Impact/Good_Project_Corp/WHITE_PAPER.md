# Good Project Corp — White Paper

> *Philanthropic organizing structure with structural solidarity mechanics — not virtue, but architecture.*

---

## 1. The Design Constraint

> If an organization's solidarity depends on the virtue of its current members, it will fail when those members leave or are replaced by less virtuous ones.
> **The structure must enforce solidarity without relying on virtue.**

---

## 2. Architecture

- **Legal entity:** Philippines (SEC registration in progress)
- **Treasury governance:** Fair BEAT rules (automatic redistribution, auditable)
- **Decision-making:** Trustnet-weighted voting (earned voice, not purchased)
- **Individual control cap:** No single entity controls >5% of treasury allocation unilaterally
- **Coordination:** Monthly alignment sessions with 4Boon

---

## 3. The Solidarity Mechanism

The governance rules ARE the solidarity. They are binding, structural, and non-optional — not an aspirational policy document.

Every structural decision is tested against the CODE 1149 design test:
> Can a bad actor extract value and block redistribution? If yes → redesign.
