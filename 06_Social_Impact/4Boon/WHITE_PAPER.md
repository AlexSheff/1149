# 4Boon — White Paper

> *Heart-to-heart assistance protocols — directional aid that reaches people, not institutions.*

---

## 1. The Failure of Institutional Aid

Charities, NGOs, and government welfare programs share a common architectural flaw: **the recipient is passive**. Aid flows from institution to person through bureaucratic channels that extract efficiency, dignity, and agency from the recipient.

4Boon's hypothesis: if you remove the institution entirely and create direct, verified, trust-weighted connections between people who want to help and people who need help — the aid is more effective, more dignified, and more efficient.

---

## 2. Protocol

```
Person in need posts a specific, verifiable need
  → Trustnet identity verified (prevents fraud)
  → Community reviews and endorses (trust-weighted)
  → Direct donors respond to specific need
  → Fulfillment verified by community witnesses
  → Donor and recipient both gain trust score
  → No money passes through 4Boon
```

---

## 3. The Heart-to-Heart Principle

Every exchange is between two people. 4Boon provides the trust infrastructure, not the resources. This preserves dignity on both sides: the person receiving help is not a charity case, they are a community member accessing a community resource. The person helping is not a donor, they are fulfilling a specific, verified request.

---

## 4. Solidarity Mechanics

Aid that requires no institutional extraction — 100% of the value reaches its target. 4Boon earns nothing from transactions. Its sustainability comes from Trustnet score improvements for verified mutual-aid participants, which feeds back into their City Quest access and Fair BEAT distributions.

---

## 5. Relationship to Good Project Corp

4Boon = the impact investment arm (capital and resources)
Good Project Corp = the civic organizing body (people, processes, governance)

Both are required. Neither can function fully without the other.
