# Pie Project — White Paper

> *Land tokenization and resource economy — transforming physical territory into participatory economic infrastructure.*

---

## 1. The Problem

Land is the ultimate non-replicable resource. Every economic inequality eventually traces back to differential access to land. Yet land ownership in most jurisdictions is:
- Opaque (title records are hard to access or verify)
- Exclusionary (requires capital or inheritance)
- Static (value accrues passively to owners, not to the community that creates it)

The value of land in Dumaguete rises because people build roads, schools, and businesses there — but only the landowner captures that value increase.

---

## 2. The Vision

Pie Project tokenizes land into participatory fractional ownership where:
- Anyone can hold a "slice" of local territory
- Value created by community activity accrues to the collective, not just landowners
- Governance of land use is weighted by usage and contribution, not by wealth

---

## 3. Mechanism

```
Land parcel → Tokenized into 1000 units
Community members earn units through:
  - Civic participation (Choose&Own actions)
  - Labor contribution (JobAround jobs)
  - Ecological stewardship (Recycle Economy contributions)
Rent/appreciation → distributed to unit holders
Land use decisions → governance by unit holders (weighted by units)
```

---

## 4. Solidarity Mechanics

Land value created by community effort returns to the community that created it. The mechanism makes extraction of community-created value structurally difficult — not illegal, but architecturally inefficient.

---

## 5. Dumaguete Application

Initial pilot: a shared community workspace or urban garden in Dumaguete.
Small enough to test the mechanics. Large enough to prove the model.
