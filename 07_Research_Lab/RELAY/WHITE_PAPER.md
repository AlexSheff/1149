# RELAY — White Paper

> *The AI-Native Professional Network Where Bots Work For You*

---

Executive Summary
RELAY is an AI-native professional network that inverts the traditional LinkedIn model. Instead of expecting users to curate profiles, broadcast availability, and manually chase connections, RELAY deploys a personal AI agent on behalf of each member — one that listens, matches, negotiates, and delivers only the highest-value human touchpoints.

The onboarding experience takes under five minutes: a conversational bot interview replaces form-filling, automatically constructing a dynamic skill graph, work preferences, and autonomy configuration. From that moment, the member's agent operates continuously — answering inbound queries, surfacing relevant problems, and routing qualified connections — while the human focuses on actual work.

RELAY's core thesis: professional value is destroyed not by lack of connections, but by the cost of managing them. AI eliminates that cost entirely.

1. The Problem
1.1  LinkedIn Is Built for a World That No Longer Exists
LinkedIn was designed for a 2003 paradigm: stable jobs, corporate hierarchies, and slow-moving career ladders. Today, the global workforce is increasingly composed of independent professionals, portfolio workers, digital nomads, and AI-augmented one-person companies. For these people, LinkedIn creates more friction than value:

Profile maintenance is a second job — requires constant manual updating to remain relevant
Cold outreach is overwhelming — inboxes are flooded with irrelevant connection requests and recruiter spam
Search is keyword-dependent — it cannot understand nuanced expertise or contextual availability
The human-to-human matching process is slow, expensive, and biased toward visibility rather than fit
High barrier to participation — low engagement from experts who have no time to "work" the platform

1.2  The Hidden Cost of Connection Management
Studies consistently show that professionals spend 2–4 hours per week on networking activities that do not directly generate value — writing connection requests, filtering irrelevant outreach, scheduling introductory calls that go nowhere, and updating profile information. For a $100/hr consultant, this represents $10,000–$20,000 per year in lost billable time.

The bottleneck is not the lack of matching opportunities. It is the human bandwidth required to manage the matching process itself.


1.3  The Opportunity
Large language models, combined with real-time graph databases and autonomous agent frameworks, have made it technically and economically viable to offload virtually all routine professional matching tasks to AI. What once required a human recruiter or business development team can now be performed continuously, at scale, and at near-zero marginal cost.
RELAY is built to capture this opportunity.

2.2  The Bot Interview — Onboarding in 4 Minutes
Traditional networks require users to invest significant time upfront before receiving any value. RELAY flips this. Within one conversational session, the bot constructs:

A structured competency graph with inferred proficiency levels and adjacent domains Skill Taxonomy
Work capacity, response time expectations, preferred engagement types Availability Model
Rate ranges, project minimums, and negotiation boundaries Pricing Logic
How much the agent is authorized to do independently vs. when to escalate Autonomy Configuration
Formal/informal, language preferences, preferred channels Communication Style

This profile is not static. The agent continuously refines its understanding based on interactions, accepted/rejected opportunities, and explicit feedback from the member.

The agent is not a chatbot that answers questions. It is a persistent digital representative that earns reputation on your behalf.
