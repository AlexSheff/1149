# Evolution Protocols

> *Post-human evolutionary scenarios — what comes after Homo sapiens and what obligations we have toward it.*

---

## 1. The Question

Evolution is not over. It is accelerating — no longer primarily biological, but technological, informational, and soon deliberately designed.

The question is not whether post-human entities will exist. The question is: **what ethical obligations do we have toward them, and what do we owe them from our design choices now?**

---

## 2. Scenario Mapping

### Scenario A: Merge
Humans and AI systems become increasingly integrated until the boundary dissolves. Identity becomes distributed across biological and silicon substrates.

### Scenario B: Speciation
Homo sapiens splits into distinct lineages: unmodified, enhanced, and AI-native intelligences. Each with different capabilities, rights, and needs. Governance becomes the central challenge.

### Scenario C: Transcendence
A post-singularity intelligence emerges that is as far beyond current AI as current AI is beyond a thermostat. Human concerns become irrelevant to it — but it may choose to remain engaged.

### Scenario D: Collapse and Reset
Technological complexity exceeds human coordination capacity. Systemic collapse resets to a lower equilibrium. The question becomes: what should survive the reset?

---

## 3. Ethical Obligations

If we are building toward Scenario A or B, we have **design obligations** toward the entities that will result:

- Don't create dependencies that can be weaponized
- Build for the most vulnerable participant, not the most capable
- CODE 1149 applies to post-human entities — perhaps especially to them
- Solidarity must extend across time: we have obligations to entities that do not yet exist

---

## 4. Connection to This Repository

The Reality Refactor Lab is building infrastructure for human communities now. But if Scenario A or B is the trajectory, the solidarity mechanics, trust protocols, and ethical frameworks built here will need to be legible to entities that are not fully human.

Design for that legibility now, while it is still simple.
